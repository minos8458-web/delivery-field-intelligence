# Project Vision

## Problem

Experienced delivery workers accumulate large amounts of operational knowledge that ordinary delivery software does not preserve well: building access quirks, cart movement constraints, parking realities, elevator behavior, time-window congestion, and efficient local sequences.

This knowledge is often tacit. It remains in the worker's memory, is inconsistently recorded, and is difficult to transfer or reuse systematically.

## Product thesis

A delivery worker's repeated field observations can be converted into structured, user-confirmed operational memory. Over time, the accumulated records can support pattern detection and context-aware decision support.

## Differentiation

DFI does not begin with route optimization.

Existing route tools primarily answer:

> What is the best route for today's stops?

DFI begins with:

> What did this driver learn here before, and when should that experience be recalled?

## First user

The founder is the first active field user. This enables direct daily validation without inventing hypothetical delivery problems.

## Success conditions for early validation

1. A field note can be captured in under 20 seconds.
2. The structured draft requires minimal correction by the driver.
3. Confirmed memories can be found later using natural location or operational terms.
4. The driver voluntarily records repeated field observations during real work.
5. At least one recurring operational decision is measurably improved by recalled memory.

## Anti-goals

- Building features only to resemble an AI grant application
- Rebranding a generic chatbot as delivery AI
- Competing head-on with mature route optimization products before evidence exists
- Collecting sensitive delivery information without a clear minimum-data policy
